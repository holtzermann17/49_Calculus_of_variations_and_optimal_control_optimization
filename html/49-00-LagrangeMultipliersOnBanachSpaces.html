Lagrange multipliers on Banach spaces


Let U be open in a real Banach space X, and Y be another real Banach space. Let f:U→ℝ and g:U→Y be continuously differentiable functions.

Suppose that a is a minimum or maximum point of f on M={x∈U:g⁢(x)=0}, and the Fréchet derivative D⁡g⁢(a):X→Y is surjective. Then there exists a Lagrange multiplier vector λ∈Y* such that

D⁡f⁢(a)=D⁡g⁢(a)*⁢λ=λ∘D⁡g⁢(a).

(The function D⁡g⁢(a)*:Y*→X* denotes the pullback or adjoint by D⁡g⁢(a) on the continuous duals, defined by the second equality.)

If X and Y are finite-dimensional, writing out the above equation in matrix form shows that λ really is the usual Lagrange multiplier vector. The condition that D⁡g⁢(a) is surjective means that D⁡g⁢(a) must have full rank as a matrix.
